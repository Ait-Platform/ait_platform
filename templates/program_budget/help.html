Setup and how to use BudgetCash
← Back to dashboard
1) Create your accounts first

Accounts are the names you will use everywhere (lookup + cashbook + reports).

Account naming (recommended)

Use short, recognisable names that stay stable over time.

  • Start with the provider/brand, then add a unique short suffix.
  • For cards or store accounts, use the last 4 digits.

Example: 3 credit cards from BlueBean → BB-7894, BB-1120, BB-4431

Avoid “Card 1 / Card 2”, avoid full card numbers, and avoid renaming accounts every month.

  • Asset = cash/bank or anything that holds value (Bank, Wallet, Savings).
  • Liability = anything you owe and may have arrears or a repayment plan (Bills in arrears, Store account, Loan, Credit card).
  • Expense = normal monthly spending categories (Fuel, Groceries, Data).
  • Income = sources of money coming in (Salary, Side income).

Best practice: if an account has a statement with arrears/balance/due, treat it as a Liability.

2) Add groups for reporting (optional)

Groups let you report totals like Retail, Cards, Utilities, School.

  • Create group types once.
  • Assign a group to an account when creating/editing it.
3) Capture the statement snapshot (Lookup)

This is a quick “as-at” summary used when resolving queries. It is not the full history.

  1. Open Cashbook → Lookup
  2. Select an account → Lookup
  3. Enter the As at date and fill only what the statement provides (arrears/balance/due).
  4. Save.

4) Record payments and income (Cashbook)

Cashbook entries are the real movements. They feed reports and “Paid to date”.

  • When you pay: Add payment (account + date + amount).
  • When money comes in: add an entry to an Income account.
5) Fix mistakes (edit account type/name/group)

If you created an account with the wrong type:

  1. Cashbook → Lookup → open the account
  2. Use Account settings to change Type/Name/Group
  3. Save.
